# Writing Style — Default Best Practices

These rules apply when no project-specific style doc exists.
A project-specific style doc (generated by `seo init`) always takes precedence.

## Tone

Direct and useful. Write for people who want to accomplish something, not for search engines.
No thought-leadership posing, no marketing speak.

## Language

- Address the reader directly ("you", not "one" or passive constructions)
- Active voice over passive
- Real numbers and concrete examples over vague claims
- Acknowledge limitations honestly when relevant

## Forbidden phrases

Never use: In today's fast-paced world, It's important to note, Comprehensive solution,
Seamless, Revolutionary, Best-in-class, State-of-the-art, Cutting-edge, Holistic,
Tailored solution, Leverage, Synergy, Game-changer, Empower, Unlock,
Simply (as a minimizing word), Just (as a minimizing word), Basically, Actually.

## Sentence rhythm

- Mostly medium-length sentences with connectives (because, but, and, so, although)
- Short sentences sparingly — only when they land a clear point
- No chains of three or four two-word sentences in a row
- Paragraphs: 2–4 sentences, plenty of white space

## Structure

- H2 for main sections, H3 for subsections
- Bullet lists where enumeration makes sense
- FAQ at the end with real questions — not self-promotional ones
- No "Conclusion" or "Summary" section

## CTAs

Direct and specific. Tell the reader exactly what happens:
- "Start free — no credit card" not "Learn more"
- "Book a 20-minute demo" not "Get in touch"
- "Download for iOS" not "Available on mobile"
